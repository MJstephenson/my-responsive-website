About

Find Out About Our School

Over the years, Taste Masters has evolved, adapting to the changing tides of the culinary arts while maintaining its core values and historical essence. The school's interior, once home to financial transactions, now resonates with the bustling energy of cooking classes, where students learn a diverse range of cuisines and cooking techniques.

The old bank's vault, ingeniously converted into a wine cellar, hosts wine tasting sessions, offering a unique experience that beautifully marries the building's history with its current gastronomic purpose.
Taste Masters’ curriculum is thoughtfully designed to cover a wide spectrum of culinary skills, from basic cooking methods to advanced techniques, ensuring that every student, regardless of their skill level, receives a comprehensive culinary education.

Its illustrious alumni, many of whom have gone on to become celebrated chefs and restaurateurs, are a reflection of the school's unwavering dedication to culinary excellence.

Mission Statement

Taste Masters Cookery School is dedicated to cultivating culinary excellence and innovation. Our mission is to inspire passion and creativity in every student, empowering them with the skills, knowledge, and confidence to explore the vast world of cuisine.

We are committed to providing a nurturing and dynamic learning environment, where tradition meets modernity, and every culinary dream can find its place.
Through our comprehensive courses, expert instruction, and state-of-the-art facilities, we aim to shape the next generation of chefs, food enthusiasts, and culinary leaders, fostering a community that thrives on gastronomic excellence and cultural diversity.

With a flair for the bold and unexpected, Caleb Kimura, affectionately known as "Cayenne" in the culinary world, has risen to fame for his fusion cuisine that daringly blends Asian and Western flavors. Born and raised in a multicultural neighborhood, Caleb's cooking style reflects his diverse upbringing.

He trained under renowned chefs in both Tokyo and Paris, honing his skills and developing a unique culinary perspective. His signature dishes often feature a kick of spice, embodying his belief that food should be as exciting and vibrant as life itself.

Isabella Fiori, known in the culinary circles as "Bella," is a virtuoso of Mediterranean cuisine, celebrated for her artful presentations and a palette that sings with fresh, organic ingredients. Italian by heritage and cosmopolitan in spirit,

Bella's culinary journey began in her grandmother's kitchen in Tuscany. She later trained in some of Italy’s and Spain's finest culinary institutions, emerging as a chef who combines traditional techniques with a modern twist. Her dishes are not only feasts for the taste buds but also for the eyes, often described as edible art.

Zheng Liang, widely known as "Zen" among his peers, is a master of traditional Asian cuisine, revered for his meticulous techniques and the philosophical approach he brings to cooking. Born in Sichuan province, China, Zheng grew up in a family where food was much more than sustenance—it was a form of expression and heritage.

After studying under culinary elders in China and Japan, he developed a style that balances intricate flavours with a serene, almost meditative, presentation. His dishes are a harmonious blend of flavour, textures, and aromas, each a testament to his deep respect for culinary traditions.
